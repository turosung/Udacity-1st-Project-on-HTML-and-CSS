Slow Loris

Slow loris is one of the primites with the longest tongues which it uses to drink nectar.

  • Scientific Name: Nycticebus pygmaeus
  • Habitat: Evergreen forests and tropical rainforests
  • Average Lifespan: 25 years
  • Average Length: 10.4 to 15 inches

This animal is referred to as a slow loris because it is an extremely slow moving arboreal creature.
Slow lorises are nocturnal; during the day they sleep rolled up in a ball,
then at night they search for food.
They are located in southeastern Asia, across India and the Philippines.
They have opposable thumbs. These thumbs (on all four legs) help them grip branches.
Their muscles allow them to remain still for long hours at a time
